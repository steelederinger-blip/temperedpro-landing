§ 02 Full Exam

Built for the 180-question reality.

The PMP exam runs 180 questions across three 60-question sections, with optional 10-minute breaks between them. Most candidates have never sustained focused decision-making for that long.

The Full Exam Simulator replicates the format end to end — same question count, same section structure, same break logic — so exam day isn’t the first time you experience it.

  • Three sections of 60 questions across all PMBOK domains
  • 3:50:00 countdown timer (pauses during breaks)
  • Flag questions for review before submitting
  • No feedback during the exam — full review after
  • 70% practice benchmark (PMI does not publish official scoring)

§ 04 The Approach

Three layers of preparation.

Knowing the material isn’t the same as performing on the exam. The path that works builds depth, then endurance, then judgment — in that order.

01

Practice with focus

Use Quiz Mode and the Terms Matching Game to build domain depth and term recall. Filter to your weakest topics and drill them until they stick.

02

Simulate exam day

Run the 180-question Full Exam to build pacing and stamina under time pressure. Practice the section structure and the optional breaks too — they matter.

03

Apply judgment

Work through the five scenario simulations to practice the situational decision-making the PMP exam emphasizes. Different industries, real consequences.
